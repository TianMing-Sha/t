Mitigating Cache Noise in Test-Time Adaptation for Large Vision-Language Models

ICME 2025 (submitted)

This study introduces a novel zero-shot test-time adaptation (TTA) method called "Cache, Residual, Gaussian" (CRG) for large vision-language models, addressing the performance degradation caused by distribution shifts in downstream tasks. The CRG method optimizes the quality of cached features through learnable residual parameters and incorporates Gaussian Discriminant Analysis (GDA) to dynamically model intra-class feature distributions, thereby mitigating the impact of noisy features. Experimental results demonstrate that CRG outperforms state-of-the-art TTA methods across 13 benchmarks, showcasing exceptional robustness and adaptability.
